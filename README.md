# 🚴 Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis (2025 – May 2026)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project was completed as part of the **Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate Capstone Case Study**.Cyclistic is a bike-share company in ChicagoThe objective was to analyze Cyclistic bike-share trip data and identify how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently. The insights generated from this analysis support Cyclistic's goal of increasing annual memberships.

---

## 💼 Business Understanding

### Business Problem
* **The Goal:** Increase annual memberships because members are far more profitable than casual riders.
* **The Strategy:** Convert current casual riders into members rather than targeting all-new users.
* **The Barrier:** Casual riders and members have completely different usage habits.
* **The Solution:** Analyze behavioral differences to build targeted, data-driven marketing campaigns.

### Business Question
* **How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?**

---

## 🛠️ Data Cleaning and Preparation
The following data preparation steps were performed in R:
* Imported and combined monthly trip datasets
* Checked for duplicate records
* Handled missing values
* Converted ride length into seconds
* Created weekday variables
* Created monthly variables
* Generated summary tables for analysis

---

## 🔍 Analysis Performed
1. **Ride Count Analysis:** Compared the number of rides taken by annual members and casual riders.
2. **Ride Length Analysis:** Analyzed average ride duration for each rider type.
3. **Weekly Usage Analysis:** Examined riding patterns across weekdays and weekends.
4. **Hourly Usage Analysis:** Analyzed ride activity by hour of the day.
5. **Monthly Usage Analysis:** Evaluated seasonal trends in bike usage.
6. **Bike Type Preference Analysis:** Compared preferences for electric and classic bikes.
7. **Top Station Analysis:** Identified the most frequently used stations.
8. **Member vs Casual Comparison:** Compared overall riding behavior between the two rider groups.

---

## 💡 Key Findings
* **Ride Counts:** Members generated more rides than casual riders.
* **Duration:** Casual riders had longer average ride durations.
* **Weekly Patterns:** Members were more active during weekdays, while casual riders were more active during weekends.
* **Seasonality:** Ridership increased significantly during warmer months.
* **Preferences:** Electric bikes were the preferred bike type among both rider groups.
* **Locations:** Popular stations presented clear opportunities for targeted marketing campaigns.

---

## 🚀 Recommendations
* Target casual riders with membership-focused promotions at top starting stations.
* Launch seasonal campaigns during peak riding months to convert summer casual riders.
* Promote membership financial benefits through digital marketing channels.
